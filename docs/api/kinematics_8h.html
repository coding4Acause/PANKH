Detailed Description

Contains functions for transforming coordinates and computing velocity in inertial and body-fixed frames.

These functions are essential for converting between the body-fixed frame (BFF) and the inertial (earth) frame, as well as for calculating velocity at any point on the body due to its kinematics.

Transforms coordinates from the body-fixed frame to the inertial frame.

Converts a point’s coordinates from the body-fixed frame (BFF) to the inertial frame, accounting for heaving and pitching motions about a specified rotation point. The transformation includes rotation (via pitch angle) and translation (via heaving motion).

Computes the total velocity at a point on the body’s surface in the inertial frame.

Calculates the velocity at a specified point on the body’s surface, considering freestream flow, plunging motion, and pitching motion about a rotation point. The freestream flow is assumed to be parallel to the x-axis of the inertial frame.
